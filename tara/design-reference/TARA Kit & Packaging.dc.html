KIT & PACKAGING CONCEPT

The unboxing is the proof.

A 3D-printed vial holder, a rigid presentation box, and two consumable tiers — designed so every order arrives as a considered, clinical kit rather than a padded envelope. Costed for both low-volume prototyping and at-scale sourcing.

COMPONENT 01

The vial holder

One modular 5-well block covers every order from 1 to 5 vials — a single part to print and stock. Snug Ø22 mm wells seat 3 mL vials upright; chamfered edges and a debossed TARA wordmark read as considered, not printed.

COMPONENT 01 · ALTERNATIVE

Die-cut cardboard insert

A cheaper, fully recyclable alternative to the printed holder: a folded kraft insert with punched cut-outs that grip each 3 mL vial. No printing, no plastic, and a clean, tactile "lift-the-lid" reveal — the everyday standard option that still presents well.

COMPONENT 02

The presentation box

A rigid two-piece lid box, soft-touch matte, with the holder seated in a moulded-pulp tray. The COA card and verification QR sit inside the lid, so opening the box is the first act of authentication.

The carton range

Four printed kraft cartons — 4, 6, 8 and 10 × 3 mL vials — each carrying the TARA logo on the front panel. A single visual system that scales with order size.
