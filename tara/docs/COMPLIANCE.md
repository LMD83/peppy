# Compliance framing — read before writing copy

TARA sells research peptides. Through 2026 the FDA has been explicit that a
"for research use only" label does not shield a seller once a product is
*marketed* for human use — and the FDA opened new enforcement actions against
several peptide sellers in Q1 2026 on exactly that basis. Payment processors
(Stripe, PayPal, Square) have also been de-platforming vendors in this
category. See the strategy brief for full sourcing.

This isn't a reason to soften the product — the reconstitution calculator is
the site's best feature — but it does mean every piece of copy on this site
follows two rules:

1. **Solution-preparation language, never dosing language.** "Reconstitution
   volume," "resulting concentration," "draw volume" — not "your dose," "how
   much to inject," or "take X per day." The calculator computes a
   concentration and stops there; it does not recommend an amount to
   administer.
2. **No aesthetic, anti-aging, or therapeutic claims.** No "get shredded,"
   "reverse aging," "boost testosterone." Compound summaries describe what's
   being *studied*, not what a compound *does for you*.

Every product page and the site footer should state plainly that products are
for laboratory and research use only, are not for human or veterinary use,
and are not drugs, dietary supplements, or cosmetics.

Get category-specific legal review before scaling paid acquisition or before
any copy strays from the two rules above.
