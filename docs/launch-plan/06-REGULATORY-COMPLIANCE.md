# 06 — Regulatory & Compliance (Ireland / EU)

> ⚠️ **This is the most important document and the biggest gap in the original brief.** Selling sports nutrition (food supplements) in Ireland is heavily regulated. Non-compliance can mean FSAI enforcement, product withdrawal, ASAI rulings, or fines.
>
> **This document is orientation, not legal advice.** Engage a food-law/regulatory specialist, an Irish solicitor, and an accountant before going to market. Where you see ⚠️ **Get specialist sign-off**, do exactly that.

> Related: [00 Overview](./00-OVERVIEW.md) · [03 SEO (E-E-A-T)](./03-SEO-PLAN.md) · [04 Content compliance](./04-CONTENT-STRATEGY-50-ARTICLES.md)

---

## 1. Who regulates what

| Body | Remit |
|------|-------|
| **FSAI** (Food Safety Authority of Ireland) | Food supplements safety, labelling, notification, enforcement in Ireland |
| **EU food law** | Reg. (EC) **1924/2006** (nutrition & health claims), Reg. (EU) **1169/2011** (food information/labelling), food supplements Directive **2002/46/EC** (vitamins/minerals), novel foods, contaminants |
| **HPRA** (Health Products Regulatory Authority) | Medicines & medical devices — relevant **only if** a product crosses into medicinal claims/ingredients (which you must avoid for supplements) |
| **ASAI** (Advertising Standards Authority for Ireland) | Advertising/marketing code, including influencer disclosure |
| **Revenue / Data Protection Commission** | VAT/tax; GDPR/ePrivacy |

> Food supplements are **food, not medicine**. The moment your product or claims imply it treats/prevents disease, it can be reclassified as a medicine (HPRA remit) — a serious problem. Stay firmly on the food-supplement side.

---

## 2. FSAI notification & placing products on the market ⚠️ Get specialist sign-off

- Food supplements generally require **notification to the FSAI** before being placed on the Irish market (submit product/label details). Confirm the current process and exact requirements with the FSAI / your specialist.
- As the brand owner ("food business operator") you are **legally responsible** for safety, composition, and labelling — even though a contract manufacturer makes it.
- Ensure your manufacturer operates to **GMP / ISO / HACCP** and provides documentation. Maintain a technical file per product.

---

## 3. Health & nutrition claims — the #1 risk ⚠️

Under **Reg. (EC) 1924/2006**, you may **only** make claims that are **authorised** and listed on the **EU Register of nutrition and health claims**, and only in the **approved wording/conditions**.

**Allowed (if conditions met & authorised wording used):**
- Authorised health claims, e.g. *"Protein contributes to a growth in muscle mass"*, *"…to the maintenance of muscle mass"* (authorised protein claims) — only when the product meets the conditions (e.g. minimum protein contribution).
- Authorised claims for specific nutrients (e.g. certain vitamin/mineral functions) in approved wording.

**Prohibited / high-risk — do NOT use:**
- ❌ Disease prevention/treatment/cure claims (*"cures", "treats", "prevents [illness]", "boosts immunity to fight off colds"*).
- ❌ Unauthorised or "general health" claims without an accompanying specific authorised claim.
- ❌ Claims that creatine/pre-workout "burns fat fast", "guarantees" results, or rapid weight-loss claims.
- ❌ Implying a food can replace a varied/balanced diet.
- ❌ Endorsements implying health benefits beyond authorised claims; misleading before/after imagery implying disease/health outcomes.

**Practical rule for the team:** if a benefit statement isn't on the EU Register in authorised wording, **don't say it** — reframe as education ("protein is important for…") and attribute to the authorised claim only where conditions are met.

> Apply this rigorously to **product pages, ads, social, influencer briefs, and all 50 articles** ([04](./04-CONTENT-STRATEGY-50-ARTICLES.md)). Influencers making non-compliant claims on your behalf is **your** liability.

## 4. Labelling requirements (Reg. 1169/2011 + supplements rules)

Each product label must include (confirm full list with specialist):
- Legal name "food supplement" + product name.
- **Ingredients list** (descending order) and the **name + amount of nutrients/active substances** per recommended daily dose, with **% NRV** where applicable.
- **Allergen declaration** — the **14 EU allergens** emphasised (e.g. milk in whey, soy lecithin).
- **Recommended daily dose** + *"do not exceed the stated recommended daily dose"*.
- Warning: *"food supplements should not be used as a substitute for a varied diet"* and *"keep out of reach of young children"*.
- Statement that the product is not intended for the general population where relevant; suitability notes (vegan, etc.).
- **Net quantity**, batch/lot number, **best-before date**, storage conditions.
- Name & address of the responsible **food business operator** (you) in the EU.
- Nutrition information as required.
- All mandatory info legible, in English (Irish optional), not misleading.

---

## 5. Quality assurance & banned substances (compliance + marketing asset)

- **Certificate of Analysis (COA)** per batch — composition, contaminants, microbiology. Keep on file; publishing builds trust (link from `/pages/quality`).
- **Banned-substance testing — Informed Sport / Informed Choice:** for sports nutrition this certifies products are tested for substances banned in sport. It's **both** a credibility/safety measure **and** your strongest differentiator vs. competitors and a real concern for any athlete who's drug-tested (GAA, athletics, etc.). Strongly recommended for hero SKUs.
- **Contaminants & heavy metals** within EU limits; **novel food** check for any newer/exotic ingredients (some require pre-authorisation).
- Traceability and a **recall procedure** in place.

---

## 6. Advertising & marketing compliance (ASAI)

- Ads/marketing must be **legal, decent, honest, truthful** and not mislead — same claims rules as §3 apply to all marketing.
- **Influencer marketing:** must be clearly identifiable as advertising — **#ad / "paid partnership"** disclosure. You're responsible for briefing influencers to (a) disclose and (b) **only** make compliant claims.
- No targeting/claims that exploit vulnerability; care with weight-loss messaging.
- Testimonials/reviews must be genuine and must not be used to imply unauthorised health claims.

## 7. E-commerce, consumer & data law

| Area | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| **Consumer rights (EU)** | Distance-selling rules: **14-day cooling-off/right to withdraw** (note exceptions for opened consumables/hygiene — get specialist wording), clear pre-contract info, transparent pricing inc. VAT and delivery. |
| **Returns/refunds policy** | Clear, compliant, accessible. |
| **VAT / Revenue** ⚠️ | Register for **VAT** with Revenue; determine correct VAT rate for your supplements (many supplements are **standard-rated** in Ireland — confirm with accountant). Handle cross-border VAT (OSS) if selling into other EU states. |
| **Company setup** ⚠️ | CRO registration, business banking, proper invoicing. |
| **GDPR + ePrivacy** | Lawful basis for marketing (consent for email/SMS), **cookie consent banner** with granular control, privacy policy, data-processing agreements with apps (Klaviyo, analytics), DPC compliance. |
| **Terms & policies** | Terms of sale, privacy, cookie, shipping & returns, and a **disclaimer** page. |

**Standard disclaimer (use site-wide / on content):**
> *"Food supplements are intended to supplement the diet and should not be used as a substitute for a varied, balanced diet and healthy lifestyle. This product/information is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease. Consult a healthcare professional before use if pregnant, breastfeeding, or taking medication."*

## 8. Compliance checklist before launch

- [ ] Manufacturer GMP/HACCP verified; technical file + COAs per product.
- [ ] FSAI notification completed for each product ⚠️.
- [ ] Every label reviewed against §4 by a specialist ⚠️.
- [ ] All on-site, ad, social & **article** claims audited against the EU Register (§3) — only authorised wording.
- [ ] Informed Sport (or equivalent) testing arranged for hero SKUs.
- [ ] Allergens correctly declared everywhere (labels + PDPs).
- [ ] Disclaimer + legal pages live (terms, privacy, cookie, returns, disclaimer).
- [ ] VAT registered; correct VAT applied at checkout ⚠️.
- [ ] GDPR: consent flows, cookie banner, privacy policy, DPAs with vendors.
- [ ] Influencer brief includes disclosure + claims rules.
- [ ] Recall/complaints procedure documented.

> **Critical-path reminder:** §2–§5 (notification, claims, labelling, QA) must be cleared **before** you market or sell. Build the site and content in parallel, but don't switch on commerce/ads until compliance sign-off. See [07](./07-LAUNCH-ROADMAP.md).
