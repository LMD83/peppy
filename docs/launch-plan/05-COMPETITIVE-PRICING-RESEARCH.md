# 05 — Competitive Pricing & "Undercutting" Research Plan

> You asked how to compete by undercutting competitor prices. This document is the **research methodology + a unit-economics framework + a realistic positioning strategy**. The honest headline: **don't try to win a pure price war with scaled UK players — compete on total landed cost, hero-SKU undercutting, and value/trust.**

## 2. The metric that matters: price-per-serving (not sticker price)

Tub sizes and serving sizes differ, so **sticker price is meaningless**. Normalise everything:

- **Price per serving** = tub price ÷ servings per tub.
- **Price per 100g protein** (for protein powders) = best apples-to-apples for protein.
- **Effective price to an Irish customer** = item price **+ shipping** (or distance from free-shipping threshold) **+ any customs/VAT risk** − loyalty/promo. *This is the number the customer actually pays* — and where UK competitors are weakest into Ireland.

> Your store should **display price-per-serving on PLPs and PDPs** (see [01](./01-WEBSITE-DESIGN-PLAN.md)) — it's a transparency win and a competitive weapon.

## 3. Research methodology (repeatable, lean)

### Step 1 — Define the basket
Pick ~10–15 **representative SKUs** that map to your hero categories: whey protein (1kg & 2.5kg), vegan protein, creatine monohydrate (250g/500g), pre-workout, BCAA/EAA, electrolytes, a bundle/stack.

### Step 2 — Build the tracking sheet
A simple spreadsheet (Google Sheets) — columns spec in §6 — one row per competitor SKU.

### Step 3 — Collect data (weekly/bi-weekly)
For each competitor SKU capture: list price, size, servings, **price-per-serving**, **price-per-100g protein**, current promo, shipping cost to IE + free-shipping threshold, delivery ETA to Ireland, and whether customs/VAT applies. Note promo **cadence** (UK sites run near-constant sales — the "real" price is the promo price, not RRP).

### Step 4 — Normalise & index
Compute a **price index**: your price ÷ market median for each SKU (100 = at market; <100 = cheaper). Track over time.

### Step 5 — Watch the promo game
Log how often and how deep competitors discount. If MyProtein is "40% off" most weeks, their effective price is the discounted one — plan your everyday pricing against *that*, not their RRP.

### Tools
- **Manual** (start here): the spreadsheet + a recurring calendar block. Free, accurate, gives you market feel.
- **Semi-automated**: price-tracking tools (e.g. Prisync, Price2Spy) when budget allows — auto-monitor competitor URLs and alert on changes.
- **Browser helpers / camelcamelcamel-style** for any Amazon listings.

## 4. Unit-economics framework (you can't undercut what you can't afford)

Before setting prices, know your floor:

```
Landed cost per unit  = manufacturing (white-label) + packaging/label
                        + inbound freight + duty (if any) + per-unit overhead
Gross margin %        = (selling price − landed cost) / selling price
Contribution margin   = selling price − landed cost − fulfilment/shipping − payment fees
Price floor           = lowest price that keeps contribution margin ≥ target
```

| Lever | Why it matters |
|-------|----------------|
| **MOQ / volume** | Bigger production runs ↓ unit cost — the main reason UK giants are cheap. Negotiate hard; grow volume to unlock lower tiers. |
| **Target gross margin** | Own-brand DTC sports nutrition typically targets **55–70%**. Below ~45% you can't fund shipping + acquisition + returns. |
| **Shipping economics** | Free-shipping thresholds protect margin while improving perceived value; AOV-raising bundles offset shipping cost. |
| **Promo discipline** | Don't train customers to only buy on discount. Use subscribe-&-save and bundles instead of constant sitewide sales. |

> **Rule:** never price below your **contribution-margin floor** to "win" a comparison. A sale that loses money isn't a win.

## 5. Positioning strategy: where to undercut, where not to

Pure undercutting vs. scaled UK players is a **losing game on a lean budget** — they can drop price further and longer than you can survive. Instead:

1. **Undercut on 1–2 "hero" SKUs** (e.g. whey protein — the category's price anchor that everyone comparison-shops). Accept thinner margin here to look competitive, make margin elsewhere.
2. **Win on total landed cost, not sticker price** — *"Free next-day delivery across Ireland. No customs charges. No waiting a week from the UK."* Often beats a lower UK sticker price that arrives slowly with friction.
3. **Compete on value, not price** — bundles/stacks (higher AOV, real saving), **subscribe & save** (10–15% off + free shipping → locks in repeat, beats one-off competitor price).
4. **Compete on trust** — Informed Sport certification, published COAs, full transparency, Irish-owned. Many buyers pay a small premium for assurance about what's in the tub.
5. **Price-match selectively** (optional) — match on hero SKUs only, advertised loudly, with margin protected elsewhere.
6. **Bundle to obscure direct comparison** — a "Muscle Stack" isn't directly price-comparable to a single competitor tub, which defuses the price war.

> **Net:** be *clearly fair and transparent* on price, *unbeatable on speed/convenience into Ireland*, and *superior on trust*. Selective undercutting + value framing beats a margin-destroying race to the bottom.
